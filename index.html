Homophones


Grammar overview:

Today we mainly focussed on the following:
Homophones are words that sound similar to another word but have different spellings and meanings. Understanding homophones can help you in building your vocabulary. Only if you learn the spellings and meanings of the homophones will you be able to use them correctly.



Word meaning:
Musher: A person who drives a dog sledge, usually in a race.
Sentence: The musher skillfully guided the team of huskies through the snowy terrain

Trail: A path or track made by the passage of people, animals, or vehicles.
Sentence: The hikers followed the narrow trail through the dense forest.

Iditarod: An annual long-distance sledge dog race run in early March from Anchorage to Nome, entirely within the US state of Alaska.
Sentence: The Iditarod is a challenging and exciting race that tests the endurance of both mushers and their dogs.
